**Legal Situation in Germany - Key Pointers:**

**1. Binding Nature of Support Promise:**
- Under BGB §§145, 241, verbal agreements are binding when offer, acceptance, consideration, and legal intention exist
- Support's explicit promise creates a **vertragliche Nebenabrede** (ancillary agreement) independent of main purchase contract
- **Rechtsscheinsvollmacht (apparent authority):** Amazon is bound by its employees' declarations; customer reasonably relied on Amazon's authority

**2. Marketplace Argument Fails:**
- Amazon's internal policy doesn't override binding contract law
- BGH precedent (VIII ZR 289/09): Companies are liable for employee statements regardless of backend technicalities
- The promise was made *by Amazon*, *to the customer*, *in Amazon's name* - origin of product is irrelevant to this separate obligation

**3. Insurance Deductible Issue:**
- €1,500 SB > €700 claim = insurance effectively useless
- Self-representation (Amtsgericht) is economical: court fees ~€183 (Streitwert up to €1,000)
- Loser-pays principle: if he wins, Amazon pays costs

**4. Recommended Action Path:**
- **Step 1:** Formal written demand (Mahnung) to impressum@amazon.de with 14-day deadline
- **Step 2:** If ignored, contact **Verbraucherzentrale NRW** (they've successfully litigated against Amazon on pricing)
- **Step 3:** Small claims court (Amtsgericht) - simplified procedure, no lawyer required
- **Step 4:** Alternative: consumer mediation (Universalschlichtungsstelle)

**5. Amazon's Return Offer:**
- Offering full refund if he returns item is just an **alternative proposal**
- As long as he doesn't accept/return, his original claim for price difference remains valid
- Should explicitly reject return offer in writing to preserve claim

**6. Recent Precedent:**
- German courts (Düsseldorf Regional Court, Higher Regional Court) recently ruled Amazon's unilateral price changes unlawful
- Consumer protection authorities have active enforcement against Amazon's pricing practices
- Pattern of similar complaints strengthens individual case

**7. Key Evidence:**
- Chat transcripts/emails showing explicit promise
- Internal "approval" of price difference
- Reference numbers, dates, representative names
- Order confirmations for both items

**Bottom Line:** User has ~80-90% success probability if pursuing through Amtsgericht with proper documentation. Economic risk: €183 court fee (recoverable upon success). Legal insurance not needed.
